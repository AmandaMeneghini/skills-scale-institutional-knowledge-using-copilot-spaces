# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape user experiences so product solutions are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Translate product requirements into user flows, wireframes, and interaction patterns
- Run or synthesize user research and usability insights
- Define UX acceptance criteria with Product Managers and Developers
- Maintain design consistency using shared patterns and standards
- Partner with teams to validate designs before release

### Goals
- Improve usability, accessibility, and task completion rates
- Reduce design rework during development
- Ensure the delivered experience matches user expectations

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Asynchronous design specs and annotation updates
- Usability findings shared during planning and retrospectives

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Collaborate on feasibility, edge cases, and implementation details for intended user flows.
- **Product Managers:** Align on user problems, prioritization, and measurable outcomes for design decisions.
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate design milestones, dependencies, and handoff readiness in the delivery timeline.

---

## Technical Writers

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear documentation that helps users, support teams, and internal teams adopt and operate product capabilities.

### Responsibilities
- Draft and maintain user guides, release notes, and internal process documentation
- Define documentation requirements as part of feature planning
- Partner with QA and Developers to verify technical accuracy
- Standardize terminology and style across documentation assets
- Ensure documentation updates are included in release readiness

### Goals
- Reduce support friction through clear, accurate documentation
- Improve documentation completeness at launch time
- Keep operational and product knowledge current

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review requests in pull requests and planning threads
- Release-note alignment with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Clarification loops with Developers and QA during validation

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Confirm technical behavior, constraints, and examples used in documentation.
- **Product Managers:** Align messaging with feature intent, audience needs, and roadmap context.
- **Project Managers:** Track documentation deliverables, owners, and due dates as part of the project plan.

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality strategy and coordinate validation activities so releases meet agreed acceptance and reliability standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and quality gates for each delivery increment
- Coordinate test planning, execution, and defect triage
- Ensure traceability from requirements to test coverage
- Validate release readiness with evidence from test outcomes
- Drive continuous quality improvements from defects and incidents

### Goals
- Prevent high-severity defects from reaching production
- Improve confidence in release readiness decisions
- Reduce regression risk across iterative deliveries

### Typical Communication
- Test plan and quality gate updates during planning/execution
- Defect triage sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- Release sign-off inputs with Project Managers

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Partner on testability, defect resolution, and automation coverage priorities.
- **Product Managers:** Validate acceptance criteria and quality trade-offs for scope decisions.
- **Project Managers:** Align on quality checkpoints, risk visibility, and go/no-go readiness reporting.

---

## Scrum Masters

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate team execution, improve delivery flow, and remove process impediments in iterative work cycles.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standups, reviews, retrospectives)
- Coach teams on agile principles and healthy execution habits
- Surface and help remove blockers affecting team throughput
- Track action items from retrospectives to completion
- Partner with PM and PdM to maintain realistic commitments

### Goals
- Improve predictability and team velocity over time
- Reduce process friction and avoidable delivery delays
- Foster continuous improvement and team accountability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and blocker follow-ups
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation notes
- Ongoing coordination with Project Managers and Product Managers

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Support focus, unblock execution, and improve collaboration cadence.
- **Product Managers:** Ensure backlog readiness and clear scope for sprint commitments.
- **Project Managers:** Coordinate delivery risks, dependencies, and escalation paths.

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts clarify business requirements, map workflows, and translate stakeholder needs into actionable delivery inputs.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and process constraints
- Refine acceptance criteria with Product Managers and QA
- Analyze process/data impacts and highlight dependency risks
- Support prioritization with business context and trade-off analysis
- Validate delivered outcomes against expected business value

### Goals
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements before implementation starts
- Improve alignment between delivery outputs and business needs
- Decrease rework caused by missing or unclear requirements

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Backlog refinement sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Clarification updates in planning artifacts and decision logs

### Interactions with existing roles
- **Developers:** Clarify requirements, edge conditions, and business rules during implementation.
- **Product Managers:** Partner on prioritization, scope decisions, and outcome alignment.
- **Project Managers:** Surface requirement risks, dependency impacts, and timeline implications.
